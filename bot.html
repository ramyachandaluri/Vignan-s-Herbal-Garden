Welcome to Our Botanical Garden

Explore the fascinating world of plants and nature in our Technical Botanical Garden. We offer a diverse collection of plant species from around our garden.

Featured Plants

Jatropha

Jatropha is a genus of flowering plants in the spurge family, Euphorbiaceae. The name is derived from the Greek words ἰατρός (iatros), meaning "physician", and τροφή (trophe), meaning "nutrition", hence the common name physic nut.

Bougainvillea

Bougainvillea is a genus of thorny, woody, evergreen vines, bushes, and trees that are native to Brazil, Peru, and Argentina.

impatiens

Impatiens is a genus of over 1,000 flowering plants that are native to the Northern Hemisphere and tropics.

Black Berry Lilly

The blackberry lily (Iris domestica) is a perennial plant with iris-like leaves and flowers that resemble lilies.

Elephant Creeper

Elephant creeper, or Argyreia nervosa, is a perennial climbing vine that's native to the Indian subcontinent. It's been introduced to many areas around the world, including Africa.

Chloris

Chloris is a genus of grasses in the family Poaceae, also known as finger grass or windmill grass.

Gomphrena

Gomphrena, also known as globe amaranth, is a genus of flowering annual plants in the Amaranthaceae family. The genus includes 139 species that are native to the Americas.

Jungle geranium

Jungle geranium, also known as Ixora coccinea, is a flowering shrub native to Southern India, Bangladesh, and Sri Lanka.

Clover

Clover, also known as trefoil, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae and the genus Trifolium.

Oliver Tomato

Jamie Oliver recommends sowing tomato seeds from March to May, and keeping them in a warm place like a sunny windowsill or heated propagator..

Shoeblackplant

The shoeblack plant, also known as Hibiscus rosa-sinensis, is a tropical hibiscus flower that is native to Vanuatu.

Rose

A rose is a flowering plant in the genus Rosa and the Rosaceae family. Roses are known for their beautiful flowers, which can be white, yellow, pink, crimson, or maroon.
